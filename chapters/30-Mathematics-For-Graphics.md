# Chapter 30: Mathematics for Graphics Programming

[← Back to Main Index](../README.md)

Mathematical foundations are essential for graphics programming. This chapter covers the key mathematical concepts used in real-time rendering.

---

## Subjects in This Chapter

### [30.1 Linear Algebra](../subjects/30-01-Linear-Algebra.md)
Core mathematics including vectors, matrices, transformations, quaternions, cross product and dot product, and matrix decomposition.

### [30.2 Color Spaces](../subjects/30-02-Color-Spaces.md)
Color mathematics including linear vs gamma space, HDR and color encoding, color space conversions, RGB to HSV/HSL, and luminance calculations.

### [30.3 Coordinate Systems](../subjects/30-03-Coordinate-Systems.md)
Spatial mathematics including world space, view space, clip space, screen space, tangent space, and UV space.

### [30.4 Geometry Mathematics](../subjects/30-04-Geometry-Mathematics.md)
Geometric operations including triangle operations, plane equations, intersection tests, barycentric coordinates, and normal calculations.

### [30.5 Interpolation and Curves](../subjects/30-05-Interpolation-And-Curves.md)
Interpolation techniques including linear interpolation, Bezier curves, splines, and easing functions.
